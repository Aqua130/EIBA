Statistics

A survey conducted by researchers from Monash University, RMIT University, and Goldsmith's University of London in 2020 found the following:

  • 1 in 3 respondents experienced image-based abuse
  • Younger adults are most commonly victims
  • Members of the LGBTQIA+ and ethnically diverse communities experience higher victimisation
  • Men are more commonly perpetrators than women
  • Young people are more commonly perpetrators
  • 1 in 6 respondents were perpetrators of image based abuse
  • Most common sites for distribution are social media, email, and mobile messages
  • Most victims are targeted by people they know
  • Women report greater negative impacts victimisation than men
  • Non-consensual sharing and threats have almost doubled in the last 10 years
  • A rise in technology use plays a big part in this increase
